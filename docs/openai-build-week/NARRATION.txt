Most hackathon demos show a polished result. The risk is that a beautiful cut
can still say more than the evidence supports.

SignalCut turns that into a reviewable decision before anything goes public.

This Threadloom demo has three claims. The first is linked to a real
shared-state screen. The second points to a persisted-feed screen. Both are
inspectable.

The third says the game supports solo play. No source is attached, so
SignalCut marks it Needs evidence and blocks the publishing decision with
Needs proof. No false green light.

This is deliberately modest. SignalCut verifies an explicit link. It does not
claim an image automatically proves a sentence. A person reviews the source
and decides whether the claim belongs in the cut.

The evidence tab keeps the original screens and hashes visible. The receipt
connects the evidence, chosen story, and rendered video.

SignalCut keeps a demo honest when polish starts outrunning proof.
