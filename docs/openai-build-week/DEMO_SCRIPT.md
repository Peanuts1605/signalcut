# SignalCut Build Week Demo Script

Target length: 65 seconds. The app should be filmed at normal speed with the
Claim Review tab visible for the central 30 seconds.

## Beat 0: How it was built (0:00-0:12)

Show the SignalCut title card.

Voiceover:

> SignalCut was built with Codex and GPT-5.6. Codex helped turn the premise
> into the claim review engine, review desk, adversarial tests, and this
> verified public demo.

## Beat 1: The risk (0:12-0:20)

Show the SignalCut preview, then switch to **Claim review**.

Voiceover:

> A demo can look polished and still make a claim its evidence does not support.
> SignalCut catches that gap before the cut goes public.

## Beat 2: The decision (0:20-0:37)

Show the publishing decision: **Needs proof**. Move through the two green
linked claim cards and then stop on the red unsupported solo-play claim.

Voiceover:

> Each claim has to name a source asset. These two claims are linked to real
> Threadloom screens. This third claim has no source, so SignalCut does not
> wave it through. It makes the publishing decision Needs proof.

## Beat 3: The truth boundary (0:37-0:50)

Open **Evidence**, then return to **Claim review**.

Voiceover:

> It is deliberately modest about what software can know. SignalCut verifies
> the explicit link and lets a human inspect the source. It does not pretend a
> screenshot automatically proves a sentence.

## Beat 4: The full chain (0:50-1:02)

Open **Receipt**, show the render hash toggle, then return to Claim Review.

Voiceover:

> The same bundle holds the evidence hashes, editorial candidates, rendered
> cut, receipt, and claim ledger. That makes a demo easier to trust and easier
> to challenge before a judge has to do it for you.

## Closing card (1:02-1:06)

Show Claim Review one final time.

On-screen text:

> SignalCut: proof before publish.
